--- title: Oops I Did it Again ♪ / year starts with a bang date: 2026-04-25 categories: - biking - pictures - family tags: - health - biking - mountain bike - family - phoenix - oakland - elbow break - surgery - olecranon - accident featured: 2026-oops-bang-02.webp#bottom type: post --- Bleah, so... I'm an idiot / can't be trusted around fence posts. **UNBELIEVABLY**, only 2-1/2 years from [breaking my right elbow](../2023-11-first-big-break/) in a bike crash, I break my *left* elbow, the same **damned* way... 💥 ![tracey & hunter in hospital room](../img/2026-oops-bang-00.avif) First things first. We both get respiratory viruses at XMAS/New Year's. **I** come down with RSV and HMPV viruses at the same time. They attack me so badly (possbily other factors?), I wind up in the hospital for two days, unable to breathe lying down without *massive* knife-stabbing pain in my lower left lung. My blood oxygen SpO2 is down in the 80s, which is "go get an oxygen tank cart levels"... We CAT-scan find small "non-life threatening" blood clots in both lungs. Queue blood thinners... Here Hunter is rescuing me from crappy food in my nice view Oakland/Berkeley hospital. ![tracey bike crash](../img/2026-oops-bang-01.avif) ... and only ~45 days later, after spending 1 week hill hiking slowly to rebuild lungs, then 1 week slowly hill biking, slowly more and more, back to 99% SpO2, still on blood thinners, terrified of crashing or bleeding... .. utter disaster. Just like [last time](../2023-11-first-big-break/), I get too close to a fence post -- this time with a "lasso" cable end (you can zoom in to right side of post just over handlebars). I'm travelling right-to-left in the dirt above in this picture, and the lasso catches and jerks my hanldebars severely, catapulting me down this ~6' short slope. Scrambling, I cant get my left foot unclipped, and take the entire fall on my left elbow => *right* into pavement (once again). My elbow is broken in two in this pic, but I dont know it yet. Racing through my head is "please dont let this be another break..." ![x-ray of broken olecranon](../img/2026-oops-bang-02.webp) Next morning, same Urgent Care (osteo specialist) place in Walnut Creek as last time => broken in two again! Interestingly, like last time, if I dont move the elbow, there's almost no pain. ![tracey hiking in fog](../img/2026-oops-bang-03.avif) So... I hike a lot, swapping my six-days-a-week 1-2 hours of biking, for hiking. ![tracey on bridge over stream](../img/2026-oops-bang-04.avif) Hunter and I find a new absolutely favorite hike in the North Bay, a [4-mile perfect loop](https://www.strava.com/activities/17559313854) starting with panoramic pacific ocean and SF views.
You then hike down to and then up an amazing rushing stream with waterfalls. It's part of the Dipsea Trail with a loop back on Steep Ravine Trail. I would never have hiked this with an actively broken arm, had I known how technical it can get. But we made it, even up a wood rung ladder section, me 3-limb hopping my way up. Utterly gorgeous though.. ![tracey bruised arm](../img/2026-oops-bang-05.avif) Blood thinners and half-casts. Wow, you wanna talk about even worse pain than the break itself? The painful black-and-blues run up/down my arm anywhere where the cast bottom settles. ![tracey hiking with biking friends](../img/2026-oops-bang-06.avif) We have a delightful morning with out-of-town friend Chanel. We start at our local fave cafe, then I head off and hike while they bike. We actually met up ~mid-way through! After the severe bruising & pain (like, not sleeping for 4 nights in a row bad...) they switch me to this removable exoskeleton much better setup. ![tracey in jeep](../img/2026-oops-bang-07.avif) Two weeks after the crash, I get the same surgery. Ironically, it's the same (wonderful!) surgeon, the same facility, the same anesthesiologist, the same OR head nurse, the same titanium fix (2 super thin & narrow criss-cross plates, 4 screws into ulna, 2 long screws lengthwise down the ulna from the end). ![elbow x-ray with hardware & screws](../img/2026-oops-bang-08.avif) Two weeks post-op, the x-rays look great, almost zero sign of break. I start physical therapy to get "range-of-motion" back, 6 days after this. Now we can push the PT a bit more.. ![tracey selfie showing elbow stitches](../img/2026-oops-bang-09.avif) The 7 sutures (going across the wavey cut line) come out (only takes a few seconds) at this x-ray review appointment, as well. ![tracey hiking on fire road](../img/2026-oops-bang-10.avif) I hike to end of our bike group "Thirsty Thursday" ride every Thursday, ~6 miles, mostly down amazing view fire roads for one hour, and then neighborhood roads for the second hour. You can see Mt. Diablo in the background. ![tracey & hunter on hiking trail](../img/2026-oops-bang-11.avif) Have I mentioned how amazing Hunter has been? With almost zero elbow mobility for the first four weeks after the crash, he's helping me shower and wash my hair, and so so much more.. ![three people on trail through redwoods](../img/2026-oops-bang-12.avif) My bestie and I hike every Sunday -- CA redwoods are always a wonder for the spirits! ![tracey selfie atop cow fence](../img/2026-oops-bang-13.avif) Here I'm on a Thirsty Thursday hike, and the one fence hop I have to `CAREFULING.EXE` each week. ![tracey returns to biking!](../img/2026-oops-bang-14.avif) So today, after another post-op review with x-rays 3 days prior, I **BIKED!** again for the first time since the crash 8 weeks ago. Amazing! I was nervous about how I'd do. My left arm is still stiff and imperfect. I'm wearing elbow and knee crash pads for good measure. And being ultra careful and slow. I've lost a ton of bike fitness -- but that's what regular biking is for :) Here I am "waving" to the fence post that took me out. It now has a traffic cone on it from the Joaquin Miller rangers after I mentioned my crash to them. ![tracey & hunter with wine & cheese](../img/2026-oops-bang-15.avif) So... I'm back to biking, slowly! 🎉 And. We're going to make 2026 truly amazing, by finally getting married, with our great family and friends, at a place along our favorite bike ride! 💍 (Here we are settling on wine & apps for The Event.)
